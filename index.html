A Community Celebration of America’s 250th Anniversary

Redding Celebrates America 250 is a special community event honoring the 250th anniversary of the founding of the United States.

The event will feature readings from the Declaration of Independence and a concert of patriotic music performed by the Greater Bridgeport Symphony, including works by Redding’s own Charles Ives, along with popular American music performed in collaboration with students from Joel Barlow High School.

Taking place at Joel Barlow High School, this public concert blends music, history, and community in a local celebration of a national milestone.

Related Events

In addition to our May 30 concert, our committee is partnering with local organizations on events that explore Redding’s Revolutionary-era history and build excitement for America’s 250th anniversary.

Redding Community Hiking Club • Free Program

Putnam Park History Hike

Saturday, April 11 • 11:00 am

Join the Redding Community Hiking Club and Redding Celebrates America 250 for a special history hike at Putnam Memorial State Park.

Redding’s Co-Historians Brent Colley and Bruce Nelson will discuss Redding’s role during the American Revolution, followed by a guided hike through the park led by Ellery Leary, the park’s historian.

This approximately 90-minute hike is rated easy to moderate. Advance registration is required, parking details will be shared with registered participants.

Fundraiser Event • Ticket Required

Cocktail Party & Screening of Glory

Thursday, April 23 • 7:00 pm

Join us at Greenwood Features in Bethel for cocktails, conversation, and a screening of the Academy Award-winning film Glory (1989).

The film tells the heroic true story of the Massachusetts 54th Regiment, whose courage inspired the first movement of Charles Ives’s Three Places in New England.

Tickets are $30 and include a cocktail hour and movie screening, and refreshments. All proceeds benefit the May 30th Redding 250 Concert featuring the Greater Bridgeport Symphony and student performers from Joel Barlow High School.
